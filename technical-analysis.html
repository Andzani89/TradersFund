Candlestick Analysis

Candlestick charts display price movements using "candles" that show open, high, low, and close prices for a time period.

Key Concept: Candlestick Anatomy

Each candle consists of a body (open/close) and wicks (high/low). Green/white candles indicate price rose, red/black candles show price fell.

Common Candlestick Patterns

Hammer

Bullish reversal pattern with small body and long lower wick, appears after downtrend.

Engulfing

Strong reversal signal where second candle completely "engulfs" the first.

Doji

Indecision pattern where open and close are nearly equal, signals potential reversal.

How to Trade Candlestick Patterns

  1. Confirm pattern forms at key support/resistance
  2. Wait for candle to fully form before acting
  3. Use volume to confirm strength of pattern
  4. Place stop-loss below pattern low (for bullish) or above high (for bearish)

Chart Patterns

Price formations that indicate continuation or reversal of trends, formed by price action over time.

Reversal Patterns

Head & Shoulders

Three peaks with middle highest, signals trend reversal when neckline breaks.

Double Top/Bottom

Two failed attempts at breaking resistance (top) or support (bottom).

Continuation Patterns

Flags & Pennants

Brief consolidation after strong move, typically resumes in same direction.

Triangles

Symmetrical, ascending, or descending - breakouts typically powerful.

Technical Indicators

Mathematical calculations based on price/volume used to forecast price direction.

Relative Strength Index (RSI)

Triangles

Symmetrical, ascending, or descending - breakouts typically powerful.

Momentum oscillator measuring speed of price movements (0-100 scale).

  • Overbought: Above 70 (potential sell)
  • Oversold: Below 30 (potential buy)
  • Divergence: When price makes new high but RSI doesn't

RSI Strategy

Combine RSI with trend analysis - only take oversold signals in uptrends, overbought in downtrends.

Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)

Triangles

Trend-following momentum indicator showing relationship between two MAs.

  • Signal Line Cross: MACD line crosses signal line
  • Zero Line Cross: MACD crosses centerline
  • Divergence: Price and MACD disagree

Support & Resistance

Triangles

Key price levels where buying (support) or selling (resistance) pressure emerges.

Identifying Key Levels

  • Previous swing highs/lows
  • Price consolidation areas
  • Round numbers (psychological levels)
  • Volume spikes

Support/Resistance Trading

Buy near support with stop below, sell near resistance with stop above. Watch for breakouts with increased volume.

Role Reversal Principle

When support breaks, it often becomes new resistance (and vice versa).

Trend Analysis

Triangles

"The trend is your friend" - identifying and trading with the prevailing market direction.

Trend Types

  • Uptrend: Higher highs and higher lows
  • Downtrend: Lower highs and lower lows
  • Sideways/Range: No clear direction

Trend Confirmation Tools

  1. Moving Averages (price above/below)
  2. Trendlines (connecting swing points)
  3. ADX indicator (strength measurement)

Volume Analysis

Triangles

Measures the number of shares/contracts traded, indicating strength behind moves.

Volume Signals

  • Breakouts: Should have high volume
  • Reversals: Often accompanied by volume spikes
  • Divergence: Price rising on declining volume

Volume Profile

Shows volume traded at specific price levels, revealing areas of high liquidity.
