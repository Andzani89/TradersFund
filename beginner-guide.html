1. Trading Basics

What is Trading?

Trading involves buying and selling financial assets (stocks, crypto, forex) to profit from price movements. Unlike investing which focuses on long-term growth, trading capitalizes on short-term market fluctuations.

Key Concept: Buy Low, Sell High

The core principle is simple: purchase assets at lower prices and sell them at higher prices. Successful traders develop strategies to identify these opportunities consistently.

Markets You Can Trade

  • Stocks: Shares of companies (Apple, Tesla)
  • Forex: Currency pairs (EUR/USD, GBP/JPY)
  • Crypto: Digital assets (Bitcoin, Ethereum)
  • Commodities: Physical goods (Gold, Oil)
  • Indices: Market benchmarks (S&P 500, NASDAQ)

Essential Trading Terms

  • Long: Buying expecting price to rise
  • Short: Selling expecting price to fall
  • Liquidity: Ease of buying/selling
  • Volatility: Price fluctuation intensity
  • Spread: Difference between bid/ask
  • Leverage: Borrowed capital to amplify positions

Pro Tip: Paper Trading

Practice with virtual money before risking real capital. Most brokers offer demo accounts with $10,000-$100,000 in simulated funds. Treat it like real money to develop discipline.

2. Market Types

Bull vs Bear Markets

Bull Market: Prices are rising, encouraging buying (trending upward for months/years)

Bear Market: Prices are falling, encouraging selling (decline of 20%+ from recent highs)

Market Cycles

Markets move in cycles: Accumulation → Uptrend → Distribution → Downtrend. Recognizing these phases helps time entries/exits.

Market Sessions

  • Asian Session: (Tokyo, Hong Kong, Singapore)
  • European Session: (London, Frankfurt)
  • US Session: (New York, Chicago)
  • Crypto Markets: 24/7 trading

Market Analysis Methods

  • Technical Analysis: Studying price charts/patterns
  • Fundamental Analysis: Evaluating economic/company data
  • Sentiment Analysis: Gauging market psychology

3. Order Types

Basic Order Types

  • Market Order: Execute immediately at current price
  • Limit Order: Execute only at specified price
  • Stop Order: Becomes market order when price hits level
  • Stop-Limit: Becomes limit order when price hits level

Order Execution Flow

Your broker → Exchange matching engine → Order book → Trade execution → Confirmation. Understanding this helps with slippage and fills.

Advanced Order Types

  • OCO (One Cancels Other): Links two orders
  • Iceberg: Large order split into smaller chunks
  • TWAP (Time Weighted Avg Price): Executes over time period

4. Risk Management

The 1% Rule

Never risk more than 1% of your account on a single trade. This protects against strings of losses.

Risk-Reward Ratio

Aim for at least 1:2 (risk $1 to make $2). Good trades have defined risk before entering.

Essential Risk Tools

  • Stop-Loss Orders: Automatic exit at predetermined loss level
  • Position Sizing: Calculate trade size based on account size
  • Diversification: Don't put all capital in one asset

Common Mistakes

  • Overtrading (too many positions)
  • Revenge trading after losses
  • Ignoring stop-losses
  • Using excessive leverage

5. Your First Trade

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Choose a broker/exchange
  2. Fund your account (start small)
  3. Analyze the market (use Chapter 2 methods)
  4. Determine entry/exit points
  5. Set stop-loss and take-profit
  6. Execute trade
  7. Monitor and manage position

Demo Account First!

Practice with virtual funds for at least 1 month before trading real money. Develop consistency in your strategy.

Trade Journal Template

Record every trade with:

  • Date/time
  • Asset traded
  • Entry/exit prices
  • Position size
  • Reason for trade
  • Emotional state
  • Lessons learned

6. Trading Psychology

Common Psychological Traps

  • FOMO: Fear Of Missing Out (chasing trades)
  • Revenge Trading: Trying to recover losses quickly
  • Overconfidence: After winning streaks
  • Analysis Paralysis: Too much information

The 90/90/90 Rule

90% of traders lose 90% of their capital in the first 90 days due to psychological mistakes, not lack of strategy.

Developing Discipline

  • Stick to your trading plan
  • Accept losses as part of the process
  • Take breaks after emotional trades
  • Review trades objectively
