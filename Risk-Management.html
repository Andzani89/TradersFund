Core Risk Management Principles

1%

The 1% Rule

Never risk more than 1% of your trading capital on a single trade. This ensures you can survive a losing streak and continue trading.

  • Calculate 1% of your account balance
  • Determine position size based on stop-loss
  • Protects against emotional trading

Stop-Loss Orders

Always use stop-loss orders to limit potential losses on every trade you place.

  • Set stops based on technical levels
  • Adjust for market volatility (ATR)
  • Never move stops further away
  • Consider trailing stops for winners

Risk-Reward Ratio

  • Measure distance to stop and target
  • Aim for 1:2 or better
  • Reject trades with poor ratios
  • Adjust position size accordingly

Risk Management FAQs

Why is risk management more important than finding good trades?

Even the best trading strategies have losing streaks. Proper risk management ensures you survive these inevitable drawdowns and remain in the game long enough for your edge to play out. Many traders find profitable setups but fail because they risk too much on each trade.

How do I determine where to place my stop-loss?

Stop-losses should be placed at logical technical levels where your trade thesis would be invalidated. Common approaches include:

  • Below support/resistance levels
  • Beyond recent swing highs/lows
  • Using ATR (Average True Range) multiples
  • Beyond key moving averages

The key is to place it where the market structure changes, not based on arbitrary dollar amounts.

What's better: fixed dollar risk or percentage risk?

Percentage risk (like the 1% rule) is generally better because it scales with your account size. As your account grows, your position sizes grow proportionally, and vice versa if your account shrinks. Fixed dollar amounts don't adapt to changing account sizes.

How do I handle risk during high volatility periods?

During high volatility (earnings, news events, market crashes):

  • Reduce position sizes to maintain the same dollar risk with wider stops
  • Consider sitting out until volatility normalizes
  • Use volatility-adjusted indicators like ATR for stops
  • Avoid trading around scheduled high-impact news if you're not experienced
Should I risk more when I'm on a winning streak?

No. Increasing risk after wins is a form of gambling mentality ("I'm hot right now"). Professional traders maintain consistent risk regardless of recent performance. The only time to increase risk is when you've formally grown your account enough to justify larger position sizes while maintaining the same percentage risk.
